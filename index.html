Background Info
Since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, AAPI individuals have experienced exponentially more racist violence than in previous years. Hate crimes driven by anti-Asian sentiment increased 1,900 percent in New York City in 2020, according to the NYPD. Between March 19 and December 31, 2020, Stop AAPI Hate, a reporting database developed at the start of the epidemic in response to a spike in racial violence, received 2,808 reports of anti-Asian discrimination. The violence has persisted into 2021, and immediately after taking office in January, President Joe Biden signed an executive order condemning anti-Asian bigotry. While anti-Asian violence has occurred all throughout the world, there has been a recent spike in the United States.
